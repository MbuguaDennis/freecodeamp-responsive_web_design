Dr Narendra Modi

14th Prime Minister of India

Here is a timeline of Dr Narendara life:

  • 1950 - Born in Vadnagar, Bombay State, India (present-day Gujarat)

  • 1968 -In the early summer of 1968, Modi reached the Belur Math but was turned away, after which Modi wandered through Calcutta, West Bengal and Assam, stopping in Siliguri and Guwahati.

  • 1969-1970 -Modi returned to Vadnagar for a brief visit before leaving again for Ahmedabad.[52][53] There, Modi lived with his uncle, working in the latter's canteen at the Gujarat State Road Transport Corporation.

  • 1971 -oined a Jana Sangh Satyagraha in Delhi led by Atal Bihari Vajpayee to enlist for the battlefield during the Bangladesh Liberation War.[58][59] The Indira Gandhi-led central government disallowed open support for the Mukti Bahini, and Modi says he was put in Tihar Jail for a short.

  • 1978 -Modi received a Bachelor of Arts degree in political science from the School of Open Learning (SOL)[68] at the University of Delhi,[47] graduating with a third class.[69]

  • 1983 -he received a Master of Arts degree in political science from Gujarat University, graduating with a first class[70][71] as an external distance learning student.[72] There is a controversy surrounding his educational qualification.

  • 1987 -Modi helped organise the BJP's campaign in the Ahmedabad municipal election, which the BJP won comfortably; Modi's planning has been described as the reason for that result by biographers.

  • 1990 -Modi rose within the party and was named a member of the BJP's National Election Committee

  • 1991-1992 -However, he took a brief break from politics in 1992, instead establishing a school in Ahmedabad; friction with Shankersinh Vaghela, a BJP MP from Gujarat at the time, also played a part in this decision.

  • 2013 -n September 2013 Modi was named the BJP's candidate for prime minister ahead of the 2014 Lok Sabha election.[181][182] Several BJP leaders expressed opposition to Modi's candidature,[183] including BJP founding member L. K. Advani, who cited concern with leaders who were "concerned with their personal agendas".

  • 2018-2019 -Modi was named the BJP candidate for prime minister for the 2019 general election.[200] The chief campaigner for the party was BJP's president Amit Shah. Modi launched the Main Bhi Chowkidar campaign ahead of the general election, against Chowkidar Chor Hai campaign slogan of INC.
